  • 1865 - born in Bombay (Mumbai), India to Alice MacDonald and John Lockwood Kipling
  • 1871 - moved to the United Kingdom to receive education
  • 1882 - returned to India and worked for local newspapers
  • 1889 - headed back to London, on the way visiting San Francisco, Singapore, Hong Kong and Japan
  • 1891 - travelled to South Africa, Australia, New Zealand and India
  • 1895 - wrote a poem named "If—" - a literary example of Victorian-era stoicism (Kipling's bibliography)
  • 1907 - was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature
  • 1936 - died at the age of 70 and his ashes interred at Poets' Corner in Westminster Abbey
  • 1937 - autobiography of Rudyard Kipling named "Something of Myself: for my friends known and unknown" was first time published
  • 2010 - a crater on the planet Mercury named after Kipling by the International Astronomical Union

Mumbai, India

Mumbai is the financial, commercial and the entertainment capital of India. It is also one of the world's top ten centres of commerce in terms of global financial flow. It was known as Bombay till 1995 and happened to be the place of birth of prominent British poet Rudyard Kipling. For most updated information on City of Mumbai and places of interest for tourists please visit government website.

Westminster Abbey

Westminster Abbey, formally titled the Collegiate Church of Saint Peter at Westminster, is a large, mainly Gothic abbey church in the City of Westminster, London, England, just to the west of the Palace of Westminster. It is one of the United Kingdom's most notable religious buildings and the traditional place of coronation and a burial site for English and, later, British monarchs. Rudyard Kipling, an English journalist, short-story writer, poet, novelist and Nobel Prize winner, was burried at it's Poets' Corner, next to the graves of Charles Dickens and Thomas Hardy. For updated information on visiting Westminster abbey see it's official website.
